Dr. Tai Solarin

A godless yet selfless humanitarian in a troubled country.

Here's a time line of Dr. Tai Solarin's life:

  • 1922 - Born in Ikenne, Ogun State, Western Nigeria.

  • 1951 - Tai Solarin married Sheila Mary Tuer and they had two children, a son and a daughter.

  • 1952 - He finished a bachelor's degree in history and geography at the University of Manchester, Great Britain. Before then, he was educated in a Nigeria missionary school, served in Britain's Royal Air Force during World War II.

  • 1955 - He protested and quit working as a Principal of Molusi College after he returned to Nigeria in 1952. This happened after Molusi's governing board forced him to open school day with hymms and prayers, and march students to church every Sunday.

  • 1956 - He started his own school, calling it Mayflower School. Located in Ikenne, southwest Nigeria. He chose the "Mayflower" as the name fpr his school after the name of the ship sailed by the Pilgrims in 1620, because it evoked images of escaping persecution for a new life of freedom.

  • 1958 - Started writing for Daily Times

  • 1959 - He startes Mayflower Junior School, then briefly returned to England to pursue graduate studies at the University of London.

  • 1965 - In spite of Tai's personal atheism, he loyally fulfilled his brother's wishes by peronally overseeing his burial. His mother was a devout Christian, a member of the Church Missionary Society.

  • 1966 - He was marked for assasination due the fact that Tia as a columnist, was a relentless critic of Nigerian military rule, as well as of corruption in the government and church. But his life was saved by the January 15 military coup.

  • 1967 - He started writing for Nigeria Tribune. He also contributed to numberous other pepers in Nigeria like The Guardian. He is the only known Nigerian columnist to have a continuously running column lasting over twenty years, and he routinely wrote well over thirty articles a year.

  • 1976 - He turned the origianl Mayflower School over to the government, though it was still run under Tai's direct guidance and innovative principles until his death.

  • 1984 - This year was the worst of Tai's being jailed, which was in Jos. Lasting seventeen months, Jos ws the longest detention he had ever suffered in his life, all for simply suggesting that the military should surrender rule to the public within six months.

  • 1989 - Dr. Solarin also became chairman of the Peopl's Bank of Nigeria, a position he held until his death.

  • 1990 - He was detained regularly for similar upsetting remarks.

  • 1991 - American humanist Norm Allen, Jr. the Executive Director of African-Americans for Humanism and Public Relations Director for Free Inquiry magazine, visited Tai's school and was very impressed with what he saw there. He later wrote of the experience: I was immediately impressed by the seriousness and dedication of the students. Secular messages stressing the importance of education and self-reliance were posted all over the walls of the school. Everyone seemed inquisitive and eager to learn.

  • 1992 - Attendance at Mayflower expanded to 1,900, including over 800 girls. Mayflower Junior School had 1,300 resident students as well as 300 day students. Both schools are so much in demand that parents petition the Nigeria minister of education to get their children in.

  • 1994 - Dr. Tai Solarin died in his home.

    • I fight with an indomitable spirit, my back to the wall, defeat is for those who accept it The greatest strands of affection are woven in adversity. Leadership means suffering. The Leader, who has no marks, indelible marks to show either on his physical body or in his mind have never led. How many Socrates did Greece breed? How many Negro's did India breed? They have one each but they all had one thing in common, sense of mission. An unquenchable thirst to get things done. We need as in this instance only one courageous Nigerian to take a stand. But no Nigerian wants to offer his head to break a coconut.

    -- Dr. Tai Solarin
